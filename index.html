Beggining of the Exchange Diary

Hi folks,

I finally decided to start my exchange “diary” (which is more likely an exchange blog) before I return from India, where I am currently doing my exchange, back to my home country Sovakia.
As an exchange student, I have made two major mistakes, which I want you as future exchange students to be aware of, because they’re crucial to your exchange experience and memories. Let me describe these points to you little more.

A diary

What I regret the most, and what no sane exchange student should forget, or be lazy to do, is taking a paper diary. Firstly when you arrive to your host country, it will be like Alice coming for her first time to the Wonderland. Everything will be very new and exciting to you, you will gain lots and lots of experience and possess constant happiness. Unfortunately, your memories of those days won’t last for very long, because there will be constantly coming new experiences and discoveries to you. That time, if you don’t take a diary, íf you don’t write down everything you have seen or discovered immidiately or at least you don’t sit in the evening to make a romance-size note of your lovely passed day, your memories will be lost, or in the best case blurred.

I went for my exchange irresponsibly if not punishably unprepared, and after coming to India I was so excited I didn’t even think of taking a diary.

  • So the very first rule: Whatever experience you get in the first months of your exchange, please take down a note of everything whatever happens to you, so you will not lose your most important life memories and won’t be regretting it later.

Lots of photos

Another thing was, kinda technical issue in my case, that I didn’t use my money in the right way and though, I went for my exchange absolutely technicaly unprepared – shitty old phone with constantly discharged battery and a low quality camera. Because of these circumstances, I wasn’t practically much interested in taking lots of photos photos, since most of these were ugly, blurred or dark. Summarized, I didn’t take any photos nor I did take down my experiences in a diary. What a hammer.

  • The second rule is clear: No electronics you buy before going on your exchange is more valuable than a good camera, or at least a smartphone provided with good camera (and obviously an interest in taking photos of everything around you).

Conclusion (TL;DR)

Exchange is the most valuable experience in your life. Nothing is more valuable: passing until college with _A_s nor your first sex. You will understand this once you’re on and once you return from your exchange. Exchange is not a year in your life, it is a life in one year. So, don’t waste your lifetime, it is too unique and you will ever be not able to go through same experience again.

  • Take an exchange diary, write down your detailed feelings and experiences,
  • Take a lot of photos while you find things around you interesting. After half the year of your exchange everything around will become absolutely ususal to you.
  • Adjust & enjoy your exchange!

went-sleep.php

went-sleep.php is a little utility written in PHP, which is supposed to help me with going regularly early to sleep
during the extraordinary days (those are, at least for me, mostly when I write some code which I am totally excited
with, so it happens to me that I am finishing and further improving it long to the night – often till 2 am or 3 am).

Busy mind doesn’t allow you sleep

Running went-sleep.php should be the last thing, except brushing your teeth and tidying away the computer, which is
done before you go to sleep. Why? If you were doing something long to the night, you (and your mind) got pretty
engaged into it. So now even if you switch off the lights, lie in the bed and close your eyes, your mind will stay busy
collecting and ordering the information about what you just right now finished doing. So you won’t be able to sleep
and you will keep getting thoughts about how to improve the stuff you have just finished and, what’s worse, you will
actively start thinking and participate in solving the ideas in these thoughts!

That’s when you should run went-sleep.php. You will issue a simple command (went-sleep.php), which you should type
to the console yourself – to strenghten your whole concentration to it. The command runs and greets you a Goodnight.

Now that your mind is fully concentrated on what you’ve just done (this time running a simple command), it can
easily sort it out and then switch to passive mode (that’s when you’re sleeping; your mind keeps sorting the earlier
information from that day but without thoughts).
